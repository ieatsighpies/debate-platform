\section{Overview}

This chapter reports final results from a descriptive sample of $n=90$ participants across four conditions
(human-human $n=21$, firm $n=28$, balanced $n=20$, open-minded $n=21$), with complete-case model analyses at $n=89$.
The one-case difference reflects variable-level missingness under complete-case regression rules,
so descriptive summaries retain $n=90$ while model estimates use $n=89$.
The inferential flow is intentionally tiered:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item confirmatory belief-change models,
    \item secondary confidence-change models,
    \item robustness checks,
    \item exploratory mechanism and qualitative representativeness diagnostics.
\end{enumerate}

All participant-level regression models use robust standard errors, and interpretation
is based on effect size direction and confidence intervals, not on single $p$-values alone.
Consistent with Chapter 4, confirmatory models carry primary inferential weight,
while exploratory outputs are retained for hypothesis generation and design guidance.

Results are interpreted against the study's combined measurement strategy: round-level
process indicators (belief trajectory, confidence trajectory, perceived influence,
reflection, acknowledgement) are used to understand how persuasion unfolds during
debate, while debate-level pre/post self-reports provide baseline and endpoint
context for judging whether observed movement reflects meaningful stance change.

\subsection{$R^2$ Comparison Across Primary Models}

Table~\ref{tab:primary_r2_compare} compares variance explained across the primary
model sequence. Incremental comparisons ($\Delta R^2$) use model-specific baselines
reflecting the confirmatory and exploratory structure: M2 and M4 build on M1 and M2
respectively; exploratory modules (M3, M4b, M4c) are compared against their immediate
structural predecessors.
This comparison documents the incremental predictive contribution of each model domain:
treatment assignment (M1 baseline), engagement quality via reflection (M2), treatment-by-engagement interaction (M3),
participant-level and contextual controls (M4), argument quality as an alternative mechanism
(M4b, M4c), and the full specification with structural covariates like debate length (M5).

Model labels and baselines used in this subsection:
\begin{itemize}
    \item M1: Treatment only (baseline)
    \item M2: M1 + Reflection quality (vs M1, $\Delta R^2 = 0.034$)
    \item M3: M2 + Interaction (exploratory; vs M2, $\Delta R^2 = 0.022$)
    \item M4: M2 + Stance strength + Topics (vs M2, $\Delta R^2 = 0.033$)
    \item M4b: M2 + Arg quality (exploratory; vs M2, $\Delta R^2 \approx 0.000$)
    \item M4c: M1 + Arg quality without reflection (exploratory; vs M1, $\Delta R^2 = 0.027$)
    \item M5: Full model (M4 + arg quality + rounds + first\_player; vs M4, $\Delta R^2 = 0.015$)
\end{itemize}

Interpretation is anchored to pre-specified confirmatory paths (M1--M2, M4--M5)
using an effect-size-and-CI-first standard; exploratory paths (M3, M4b, M4c)
provide secondary context.

\section{Robustness Checks}

The robustness exports preserve coherence with the main model narrative:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Outlier trimming does not produce a directionally identified AI belief effect.
    \item VIF diagnostics show low collinearity among substantive predictors
    (all non-intercept VIFs in the final M5 block are near 1 and below 1.5), while the
    high M5 intercept VIF (16.34) is treated as a known coding/scaling artifact of the intercept
    term under factor contrasts and standardized covariates, not as evidence of structural predictor
    multicollinearity.
    \item Cohort-level descriptors from the summary export remain modest (Cohen's $d=0.286$, condition ANOVA $F=0.488$, $p=0.691$).
\end{itemize}

These checks are included to test model sensitivity under plausible data perturbations,
not to substitute for the confirmatory model family.

\section{Validation of GPT Quality Scoring Features}

To validate automated quality features used in downstream modeling,
I compare GPT-derived scores against double-rated human judgments
from exported validation samples.

\subsection{Reflection Quality Validation}

Using the reflection validation sample ($n=30$):
\begin{itemize}
    \item Spearman correlation (GPT vs mean human): $\rho=0.587$, $p<0.001$
    \item Human-only agreement: ICC$(2,1)=0.863$, ICC$(2,k)=0.926$
    \item GPT+human agreement: ICC$(2,1)=0.667$, ICC$(2,k)=0.857$
\end{itemize}

Interpretation: human raters show high internal consistency for reflection quality,
and GPT reflection scores show positive, practically meaningful alignment with human-ranked ordering.

\subsection{Persuader Argument Quality Validation}

Using the persuader-argument validation sample ($n=30$):
\begin{itemize}
    \item Spearman correlation (GPT vs mean human): $\rho=0.797$, $p<0.001$
    \item Human-only agreement: ICC$(2,1)=0.715$, ICC$(2,k)=0.834$
    \item GPT+human agreement: ICC$(2,1)=0.725$, ICC$(2,k)=0.888$
\end{itemize}

Interpretation: GPT-derived argument-quality features show strong monotonic alignment
with human ratings and comparable reliability contribution in this sample.

Taken together, both validation families support the current GPT quality features,
with stronger alignment for argument quality and moderate-to-strong alignment for reflection quality.

\section{Exploratory Associational Diagnostics}

AI-group pushback-confidence association is:
\begin{align}
r=-0.298, \quad p=0.2295, \quad n=18
\end{align}
This $n=18$ mechanism analysis is the AI-condition subset of the qualitative mechanism sample
($n \approx 24$ reflections) specified in Chapter 4.
Given the small AI subsample ($n=18$), I checked whether this association was driven by a single influential observation.
The leave-one-out jackknife range remained negative ($r_{jackknife} \in [-0.412,-0.169]$), but the spread indicates
small-sample instability in magnitude; this is treated as a directional signal only, not a confirmed mechanism effect.

\subsection{Exploring All P1 Variables (Associational)}

To keep exploratory interpretation conservative, I also ran additive
associational checks across collected player1 (P1) variables that were not part
of the confirmatory estimator set. These tests are hypothesis-generating and do
not alter confirmatory inference.

The currently eligible P1-variable checks are:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Influence mean association with belief change:
        $\beta_{\text{influence\_mean\_p1}}=-0.0007$, 95\% CI $[-0.3936,0.3921]$,
        $p=0.9971$, $n=90$.
    \item AI vs human group difference in influence mean (Mann--Whitney):
        mean difference $=-3.2635$, $U=612.5$, $p=0.2861$, $n=90$.
\end{itemize}

Descriptively, the P1 influence mean was $16.0787$ (SD $16.4041$, non-missing
$n=90$). Consistent with the pushback/jackknife caveat above, these all-P1
checks are interpreted as associational diagnostics for follow-up study design,
not as evidence of identified mechanism effects.

\subsection{Representativeness Caveat}

Qualitative sample proportions in the exploratory representativeness export
approximate the corresponding full-population proportions (maximum deviation $\Delta=0.045$):
\begin{itemize}
    \item Human-human: $0.250$ in qualitative sample vs $0.205$ in population ($\Delta=+0.045$)
    \item Firm: $0.250$ vs $0.284$ ($\Delta=-0.034$)
    \item Balanced: $0.250$ vs $0.274$ ($\Delta=-0.024$)
    \item Open-minded: $0.250$ vs $0.237$ ($\Delta=+0.013$)
\end{itemize}

These exploratory associational findings (pushback/jackknife, all-P1 checks,
and qualitative diagnostics) are reported as hypothesis-generating and preliminary,
with modest residual subsample imbalance.

\section{Synthesis}

The updated evidence chain is internally coherent:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Confirmatory belief models: pooled AI effects are not identified (all confirmatory CIs include 0).
    \item Secondary confidence models: pooled AI effects are not identified in confirmatory specifications (CIs include 0).
    \item Robustness checks: no sensitivity configuration yields a directionally identified confirmatory AI belief effect.
    \item Exploratory associational outputs (pushback/jackknife, all-P1 variables,
          qualitative diagnostics): findings are hypothesis-generating.
\end{enumerate}

Overall, under these model specifications and this sample, pooled AI opponent effects on belief
and confidence were not directionally identified. Exploratory diagnostics inform
next-step hypothesis design.
Chapter 6 therefore interprets these findings as pilot-stage directional patterning
under non-identified confirmatory effects, with exploratory modules treated as
hypothesis-generating rather than mechanism-confirming evidence.
