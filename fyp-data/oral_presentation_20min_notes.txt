Debate Platform FYP - 20 Minute Oral Presentation Notes

Title
- Bidirectional Persuasion in Human-AI Debates: Experimental Platform, Inference Pipeline, and Governance Relevance

0) Presentation Goal (30-45 sec)
- Explain what was built, what was measured, what was learned, and why it matters for AI governance and agentic systems.

1) Problem and Motivation (2 min)
- Core question: Do AI debate personalities (firm, balanced, open-minded) produce different belief-change patterns compared with human-human debates?
- Broader motivation: Move from anecdotal "AI is persuasive" claims to measurable, auditable influence dynamics.
- Positioning: Platform is both an intervention environment and a data-generation environment.

2) Research Objectives and Contributions (2 min)
- Objectives:
  - Build full-stack controlled debate platform for human-AI and human-human debates.
  - Enforce information hiding to reduce source-bias confounding.
  - Capture rich round-level data for bidirectional persuasion analysis.
- Main contributions:
  - Methodologically controlled debate system.
  - Fine-grained dataset (arguments, belief trajectories, confidence, influence, reflections, attribution).
  - Comparative evidence across AI personalities and human baseline.

3) System and Experimental Design (3 min)
- Conditions:
  - human-human (control)
  - firm AI
  - balanced AI
  - open AI
- Procedure per debate:
  - Initial stance/openness
  - Turn-based arguments
  - Per-round belief + confidence + influence
  - Reflection/paraphrase and acknowledgement
  - Post-debate attribution and awareness effect
- Architecture highlights:
  - React + Express + MongoDB + Socket.IO
  - OpenAI GPT-4o mini for AI arguments and quality scoring
  - Role-based access and operational controls for experiments

4) Data and Measures (3 min)
- Key variables:
  - belief_value (0-100), delta_belief, belief_shift
  - confidence, influence
  - arg_quality and reflection_quality (multi-dimensional GPT rubric)
  - attribution and attribution confidence
- Important engineering decision:
  - arg_player_role (player1/player2) derived and preserved across all conditions.
  - Rationale: submitted_by is insufficient in human-human because both are "human".
- Data quality:
  - completed-only filtering, sequence checks, transparent exclusions
  - round-0 baseline imputation with documented mapping

5) Statistical Analysis and Why It Is Defensible (3.5 min)
- Primary models:
  - 2-level human-vs-AI contrast
  - 4-level condition x round mixed-effects model
- Covariates:
  - n_rounds (exposure control), first_player_p1 (primacy confound), pre-survey openness, topic fixed effects
- Why mixed effects:
  - repeated rounds are clustered; avoids inflated significance from naive independence assumptions
- Mechanism tests:
  - moderation via opponent attribution
  - mediation via reflection quality (human side)

6) ML Pipeline and Purpose (3.5 min)
- Purpose:
  - predictive estimation for expected belief movement
  - feature signal discovery for policy design
- No-leakage design:
  - features restricted to information available at or before round r
- Core vs exploratory workflow:
  - stable core feature set
  - staged exploratory blocks with review gate before inclusion
- Evaluation:
  - GroupKFold by debate_id to prevent within-debate leakage
  - ElasticNet baseline + XGBoost comparison

7) Findings-to-Impact Framing (1.5 min)
- Practical implication:
  - Not "maximize persuasion at all costs"; optimize constrained dialogue quality.
- Agentic use cases:
  - software engineering/system-design facilitation
  - Sales Enablement Copilots
  - Product Onboarding & Activation
- Generalization strategy:
  - domain-invariant backbone (turn dynamics, confidence, reflection fidelity)
  - domain-specific adapters (norms, constraints, evidence standards)

8) AI Governance Relevance (2 min)
- This work operationalizes influence risk with measurable indicators:
  - belief shift magnitude/persistence
  - stance-flip rates
  - attribution and awareness effects
  - reflection fidelity signals
- Governance utility:
  - audit-ready metrics
  - policy guardrails (non-coercion, transparency, user agency)
  - ex-ante constraints and ex-post accountability

9) Limitations and Future Work (1.5 min)
- Current limits:
  - sample size constraints and condition imbalance
  - no longitudinal re-contact for persistence of change
  - persuasion quality metrics still evolving
- Future work from report:
  - model forecasting of stance change timing
  - semantic drift analysis for AI adaptation to human arguments
  - 48-72 hour follow-up to measure durability of persuasion

10) Strong Closing (30 sec)
- Closing line:
  - This project converts conversational persuasion from a black-box concern into a
    measurable, governable, and optimizable human-AI interaction process.


Q&A Cheat Sheet
- Why n_rounds?
  - Exposure control: 14-round debate has much more persuasion opportunity than 3-round.
- Why first_player_p1?
  - Primacy confound; known assignment imbalance in balanced condition.
- Why no-leakage emphasis?
  - Prevents inflated performance and invalid policy conclusions.
- Why arg_player_role?
  - Consistent speaker attribution across AI and human-human conditions.
- Why GroupKFold by debate?
  - Avoids train-test contamination from same debate rounds.
